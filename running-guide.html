What's Inside

Four sections. One clear path forward.

01

Running Formats for Lifters

Which types of runs actually work alongside a strength program — and which ones create more problems than they solve. Covers easy aerobic runs, tempo work, and interval training through the lens of interference effect research. Explains which formats build endurance with minimal cost to strength adaptation, and which push recovery demand beyond what a lifter's schedule can typically absorb.

02

Volume & Frequency Guidelines

How many kilometres per week you can handle at different training stages without compromising recovery. Starting points for lifters new to running, progression guidelines as fitness builds, and upper limits for different training phases. Based on load tolerance research rather than generic running plans that don't account for the concurrent strength demand.

03

Scheduling Around Strength

How to sequence lifting and running across the week so both qualities develop instead of competing. Same-day session ordering, minimum recovery windows between conflicting modalities, and weekly structures that support progress in both. Covers 3-day, 4-day and 5-day training week templates, each designed to protect the strength base while building aerobic capacity.

04

Muscle Retention Protocol

The training and nutrition adjustments that protect your strength base while building aerobic capacity. Minimum effective dose for strength maintenance during an endurance focus, protein intake targets for concurrent training, and the specific training variables most at risk when running volume increases. Practical strategies, not vague reassurances.

A Few Things Worth Knowing
  • This guide assumes you already train seriously. It skips the basics and gets straight to the decisions that matter for someone lifting 3–4x per week.
  • This is a digital PDF. You'll receive a download link by email immediately after purchase.
  • If you're brand new to running and have never built an aerobic base, the volume guidelines here are conservative by design. Start lower than you think you need to.
  • If you want the complete system — the Blueprint combines this with a full 12-week plan, recovery protocol and nutrition framework.
