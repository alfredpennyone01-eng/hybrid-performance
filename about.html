About

Built by a hybrid athlete, for hybrid athletes.

[ Your Photo ]

I started combining strength training and endurance sports the same way most people do: by accident. A few years of lifting, then a friend convinced me to sign up for a half marathon. I didn't stop lifting. I didn't want to stop lifting. So I tried to do both.

What followed was about two years of confused programming, conflicting advice, and the slow realisation that almost everything published about fitness was built for one type of athlete. The strength world assumed you weren't running. The endurance world assumed you weren't lifting. Neither had a framework for athletes who were genuinely serious about both.

The generic advice was everywhere and useless. "Just listen to your body." "Train hard, rest harder." "Balance is key." None of it told me when to run relative to when to lift. None of it explained why my legs felt destroyed two days after a long run even when I'd slept and eaten well. None of it addressed the specific problem of trying to improve your 5K time and your deadlift in the same twelve-week block.

So I started reading the actual research. The interference effect literature. The studies on concurrent training. The data on HRV and recovery. I started treating my training like a structured experiment instead of a routine, and the results were different enough that I kept going.

Hybrid Performance is what I built when I decided to formalise that work. Not a training philosophy. Not a brand. A set of resources designed around one specific problem: how do you train both effectively, structurally, and without one discipline quietly destroying the other?

Everything here is grounded in research I could actually verify, structures I've personally tested, and principles that hold up over time rather than just for one training block. The products are built to give you what I spent years assembling — the frameworks, the protocols, the sequencing logic — without the years.

I'm not a personal trainer. I'm not a running coach. I'm someone who spent a lot of time solving a problem that didn't have a clean answer, and then built the resources I wished I'd had at the start. If you train both and you're serious about doing it right, this is built for you.
